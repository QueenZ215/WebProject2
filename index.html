Unboxing your home network

Welcome to Zoë 's Network Navigations. Today, im excited to bring you an unboxing article!? But rather than me pretending to be surprised when opening a package, you get to be surprised by what devices are on your home network.

Download Nmap for windows


Nmap Windows Download by Zoë Coolbaugh

First things first, before we can expose the secrets lurking in your living room, we need the ultimate spy tool. That's right, go grab Nmap for Windows. It's free, it's powerful, and it's basically the binoculars of the digital age. You wouldn't unbox a mystery box without a flashlight, right?

  • browse to Nmap for Windows
  • Once you download the file run it and follow the install prompts (you will need admin prevlages for this part)

Open cmd.exe


Windows run window by by Zoë Coolbaugh

Now that you've installed Nmap, it's time to go full hacker mode. Open up cmd.exe like you're launching a nuclear missile. This is where the magic starts. Black box, white text, it looks scary, feels cool, and will make you feel like you're in Mr. Robot (minus the hoodie… or not).

  • we need to find out what ip address your local network is using
  • The quickest way to do that is to open cmd.exe
  • Press the windows key + r to bring up the run window
  • In the provided box type cmd and press enter

Type Command ipconfig


Windows cmd terminal by by Zoë Coolbaugh

Ready for the real juice? Type 'ipconfig' and prepare to reveal the true identity of your device on the network. It’s like checking your driver’s license before going on a spy mission: gotta know who you are before finding everyone else. Your IP address? It's your ticket to the unboxing event of the decade.

  • once Cmd.exe is open type in ipconfig
  • look for your active connection to a netowrk either ethernet or wireless lan
  • under your active connection look for IPv4 Address and Subnet mask
  • Save both of these for the next step

Open Zenmap


Zenmap action shot by nmap.org

Let's make things visual, baby. Launch Zenmap and be dazzled. This is Nmap's flashy sibling, which turns scans into visually appealing graphs and colorful maps. If Nmap is a detective, Zenmap is the conspiracy wall with red strings. Click that icon and get ready to see everything.

  • Back to your desktop and look for the new shorcut named "Nmap - Zen map GUI"
  • Double click to run that shortcut

Run scan and View results


Zenmap scan results by Zoë Coolbaugh

Here, it is the moment of truth. Hit scan and watch as your network secrets spill out like confetti from a busted piñata. Printers you forgot existed, your smart fridge, your neighbor’s weird Wi-Fi name, it’s all here. Congratulations. You've officially unboxed your home network.

  • Here is where we are going to use the IPv4
  • Put your ip address and mask in the target box
  • there are several options for scans in the profile menu
  • once the scan runs Nmap will give you all kinds of information for the things on your network
